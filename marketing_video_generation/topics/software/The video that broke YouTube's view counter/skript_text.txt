==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
In 2014, PSY's "Gangnam Style" passed 2,147,483,647 views — exactly the max of a
signed 32-bit integer (2³¹−1, ~2.147 billion). YouTube's counter couldn't go
higher, so Google rebuilt it as 64-bit (max ~9.2 quintillion). Same bug family as
the Boeing 248-day overflow: a counter that ran out of room.
Punchline: a pop song got too popular for the math — every counter has a ceiling,
and someday something hits it.

KEY VISUALS:
- A view counter racing up, then jamming at 2,147,483,647.
- A "32-bit → 64-bit" upgrade: the ceiling rocketing far out of reach.

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

In 2014, a song got so popular it broke YouTube.
[A view counter spinning wildly, then slamming to a dead stop.]
Gangnam Style hit a number the site literally could not count past — and Google
had to rebuild the view counter from scratch. Here's the limit a music video
crashed into.
[The counter frozen at 2,147,483,647.]

--- THE HIDDEN CEILING (≈0:12–0:38) ---

For years, YouTube stored each video's view count as a 32-bit number. And a number
like that has a hard ceiling — about 2.1 billion.
[A bar labeled "2,147,483,647" — the wall.]
Computers store numbers in a fixed amount of space. A 32-bit counter simply can't
go higher than two-point-one billion — there's no room for a bigger number. And for
a long time, that was fine. No video on Earth had ever come close. Two billion
views felt impossibly far away.

--- THE SONG THAT BLEW PAST IT (≈0:38–1:00) ---

Then Gangnam Style went supernova.
[The video exploding in popularity worldwide.]
It blew straight past 2.1 billion views — and the counter hit its ceiling. There
was simply no bigger number for it to show.
[The counter stuck, glitching at the max.]
A pop song had become too popular for the math.

--- THE FIX (≈1:00–1:18) ---

So Google upgraded the counter to 64 bits. The new ceiling? Around 9.2 quintillion.
[The ceiling rocketing upward, off the screen.]
That's over a billion times bigger — enough for every person alive to watch a single
video a billion times each. Problem solved... for a very, very long time.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:18–1:30) ---

It's a funny story, but it's a real lesson: every counter has a ceiling.
[A row of counters, each with a limit line.]
Build something popular enough, run it long enough, and eventually — something
always hits the wall.
