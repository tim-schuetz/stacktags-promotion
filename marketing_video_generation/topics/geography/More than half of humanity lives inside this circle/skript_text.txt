VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: More than half of humanity lives inside this circle
Topic: Geography
Target length: ~85 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The "Valeriepieris circle": center it near Guiyang in southern China with a radius
of ~4,100 km, and it covers only ~10% of Earth's surface — yet inside live over
4.2 billion people, MORE than in the entire rest of the world combined (all of
Europe, Africa, both Americas, etc.). Humanity is clumped, not spread.
Punchline: half of everyone alive fits inside one circle; the other half gets the
whole rest of the planet.

KEY VISUALS (fits the existing globe assets):
- A red circle laid over Asia on the globe; a live counter "inside vs outside";
  the highlighted countries inside lighting up.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:10) — the payoff up front ---

More than half of every human being alive lives inside this one circle.
[A red circle drops onto the globe over Asia; a counter starts: "inside vs outside".]
Draw it on the map, and there are more people in here than in the entire rest of
the world — combined.
[The "inside" counter races past the "outside" one.]

--- THE CIRCLE (≈0:10–0:35) ---

Center it near Guiyang, in southern China, with a radius of about 4,100
kilometers.
[A dot near Guiyang; the circle expands out to ~4,100 km.]
That circle covers only around a tenth of the Earth's surface. But inside it live
over 4.2 billion people.
[Counter inside: 4,200,000,000+.]
Outside it — and that's everywhere else: all of Europe, all of Africa, both
Americas, the whole rest of the planet — there are fewer.
[The entire outside world dimmed, its counter clearly lower.]

--- WHY (≈0:35–1:00) ---

It works because the circle scoops up nearly all of humanity's great population
centers at once —
[Inside the circle, countries light up one by one.]
China, India, Indonesia, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Japan, almost all of Southeast
Asia. Half the human race, packed into one corner of the globe.
[The circle glowing dense and bright against a sparse world.]

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:00–1:12) ---

We picture people spread evenly across the world.
[A globe with dots scattered everywhere — then they rush together into the circle.]
They're not. They're clumped, hard, into this one ring — more than half of
everyone alive, with the entire rest of Earth left over for the other half.
