VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: The country where half the economy is sent from abroad
Topic: Economics
Target length: ~90 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Tajikistan: in some years, remittances — money sent home by labor migrants
working abroad (mostly in Russia) — have equalled around HALF of the country's
GDP, one of the highest ratios on Earth. The money mostly funds everyday living,
not investment, so the country is deeply exposed to events far away.
Punchline: most nations' wealth is made within their borders; half of
Tajikistan's is made in someone else's.

NOTE (honesty): the share swings year to year (it has peaked near ~50%, lower
since) — say "in some years, around half." Most migrants work in Russia.

KEY VISUALS:
- A map: money arrows flying from Russia into Tajikistan; a GDP bar that's ~half
  made of "sent from abroad."

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:10) — the payoff up front ---

Imagine a country where half the entire economy isn't earned at home — it's wired
in from people working in another country.
[A GDP bar fills: half labeled "earned at home," half "sent from abroad."]
That's Tajikistan. In some years, the money its citizens send back from working
overseas has added up to around half of the nation's whole economy.
[Money arrows streaming across a border into the country.]

--- WHERE IT COMES FROM (≈0:10–0:38) ---

Most of those workers are in Russia — on construction sites, in factories, in
shops — sending their wages home to families in Tajikistan.
[A worker in Russia → cash → a household back home lighting up.]
And this money isn't building factories or railways. It's buying food, paying
rent, covering school. It keeps millions of households afloat, day to day.
[Groceries, rent, schoolbooks paid for by the incoming cash.]
The economy at home is small. The wages earned abroad are what actually feed the
country.

--- THE FRAGILITY (≈0:38–1:05) ---

Which leaves Tajikistan tied to things happening far away.
[A thread connecting Russia's economy to a Tajik dinner table.]
When Russia's economy dips, or its currency drops, or migration rules tighten,
that flow of money slows — and a recession a thousand kilometers away suddenly
lands on the kitchen table at home.
[The money arrows thin out; the household lights dim.]
Half your economy, riding on someone else's.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:05–1:18) ---

Most countries' wealth is made inside their own borders.
[A normal country: GDP bar all one color.]
Half of Tajikistan's is made in someone else's — and mailed home.
