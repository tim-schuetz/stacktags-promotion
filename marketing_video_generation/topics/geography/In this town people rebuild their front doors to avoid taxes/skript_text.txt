VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: In this town people rebuild their front doors to avoid taxes
Topic: Geography
Target length: ~90 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Baarle-Hertog (Belgium) / Baarle-Nassau (Netherlands): a town diced into a
patchwork of 20+ Belgian enclaves inside the Netherlands, some with Dutch
counter-enclaves inside them. The border runs through houses; the rule is that a
house belongs to whichever country its FRONT DOOR opens into. People have
literally moved their front doors to switch country (and tax).
Punchline: most borders are invisible lines on a map; here it's a line on your
kitchen floor — and you can change countries by moving a door.

KEY VISUALS:
- The border painted across streets/gardens/living rooms, marked with crosses
  and tiny B/NL flags on house numbers.
- A front door being physically relocated a few meters across the line.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:10) — the payoff up front ---

In this one small town, you can sit in Belgium and cook dinner in the
Netherlands — without leaving your house.
[A living room; a border line painted across the floor, sofa on one side, kitchen on the other.]
Because here, the border doesn't go around the buildings. It goes straight
through them. And one simple rule decides which country you live in: where your
front door is.
[A door glows; a "B" flips to "NL" as the camera crosses it.]

--- THE PATCHWORK (≈0:10–0:38) ---

This is Baarle — a town chopped into a jigsaw. The Belgian side is made of more
than twenty little islands of land, scattered inside the Netherlands —
[A map: Belgian patches dotted across Dutch territory.]
and some of those islands even contain smaller islands of Dutch land inside them.
The border is painted right across roads, gardens, cafés, and bedrooms — marked
with little crosses and a B on one side, an N on the other.
[Crosses on the pavement; house numbers each carry a tiny flag.]

--- THE FRONT-DOOR RULE (≈0:38–1:05) ---

So when a house straddles the line, which country is it in? Whichever side the
front door opens onto.
[A house cut in half by the border; a spotlight lands on the front door.]
And that has real consequences. When the two countries had different tax rules,
people did the obvious thing: they moved their front door a few meters — across
the line — to officially live in the cheaper country.
[The door slides sideways across the border; a tax bill shrinks.]
A doorway, relocated, and suddenly you've switched nations.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:05–1:18) ---

Two town halls, two mayors, two sets of house numbers — all in one little town.
[A split-screen of duplicate everything, B and NL side by side.]
Most borders are lines on a map you'll never see. Here, the border runs across
your kitchen floor — and you can change countries just by moving your door.
