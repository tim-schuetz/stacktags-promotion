==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Four ways professionals hedge a portfolio against a crash:
1. Short-term bonds (cushion in a sell-off — but only if interest rates aren't rising)
2. Banking-liquidity / money-market ETFs (e.g. XEON, XFFE — "smart cash" earning the overnight rate)
3. Short options as a hedge (sell premium to offset losses — what hedge funds do)
4. Short selling as a hedge (bets that profit when the market falls)
Framing mirrors the channel's signature: front-load all 4, then push back on the
usual "just diversify" advice (useless when stocks, real estate AND crypto are all
near highs together), then explain each.

NOTE (responsibility): educational, NOT advice. Flag the catch on each (bonds vs
rates; ETFs low-risk not zero-risk; shorting/short-options carry big risk).

KEY VISUALS:
- A red crashing market; a portfolio that barely dips thanks to 4 "shields."
- Four labeled shields snapping into place one by one.

SCRIPT

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — all four up front, then the twist ---

Here are 4 ways to protect your portfolio from a market crash: short-term bonds,
money-market ETFs, short options, and short selling.
[Four shields snap up around a portfolio as they're named. Tag: "educational — not advice."]
Now, this is not what most advisors will tell you. Ask a random adviser how to
protect your money, and the answer is always the same word: diversify.
[A wheel of "diversified" assets.]
But when stocks, real estate, AND crypto are all near record highs at the same
time, spreading across them stops protecting you — they can all fall together. So
here's what professionals actually do instead.

--- 1: SHORT-TERM BONDS (≈0:16–0:40) ---

First, bonds. When stocks tumble, money often rushes into safer government bonds,
which can rise and cushion your fall.
[Money flowing from red stocks into green bonds.]
The catch: bonds lose value when interest rates go up. So they really only protect
you if rates aren't climbing — and sticking to short-term bonds keeps that risk
small.

--- 2: BANKING-LIQUIDITY ETFs (≈0:40–1:00) ---

Second, money-market or "banking-liquidity" ETFs — like XEON or XFFE.
[Tickers shown as examples, not recommendations.]
These quietly park your cash at the central bank's overnight rate. Almost no price
risk, and they actually earn interest while you wait out the storm. Think of it as
smart cash — somewhere to hide that still pays you.

--- 3: SHORT OPTIONS AS A HEDGE (≈1:00–1:22) ---

Third, using options as a hedge — specifically, selling them. By shorting options
against your holdings, you collect premium income that softens the blow when prices
drop. It's a core hedge-fund move.
[A portfolio dipping; option premium income filling part of the gap.]
Powerful — but selling options carries its own serious risks, so it's an advanced
tool, not a beginner's.

--- 4: SHORT SELLING AS A HEDGE (≈1:22–1:42) ---

Fourth, short selling. You place bets that profit when the market falls — so if
your stocks drop, those gains help offset the loss.
[A short position turning green as the market turns red.]
Used as a hedge, it's like an insurance policy against your own portfolio. Just
remember: a short's losses can run high if the market climbs instead.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:42–1:54) ---

You can't stop a crash from coming.
[The red market, but the portfolio barely dented behind its shields.]
But you don't have to just sit there and take it. The pros don't only diversify —
they build in real protection. Even one or two of these can turn a brutal drop
into one you can survive.
