==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Margin trading = borrowing money from your broker to buy more than your cash
allows, using your investments as collateral (leverage). It multiplies gains AND
losses equally. If your position falls too far, you get a "margin call": add cash
now or the broker liquidates — and you can lose MORE than you put in.
Punchline: margin doesn't change whether you're right — it just multiplies the
result. An accelerator with no brakes.

NOTE (responsibility): educational, NOT advice. The "losses are multiplied / you
can lose more than your deposit" message is the key honest point.

KEY VISUALS:
- $1,000 of your cash + $2,000 borrowed = $3,000 buying power.
- A gain scenario and a loss scenario, both 3× — then a "MARGIN CALL" alarm.

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

Margin trading is how a trader turns 1,000 dollars into 3,000 dollars of buying
power — by borrowing the rest.
[$1,000 cash + $2,000 borrowed → $3,000 buying power.]
It can multiply your gains. But it can also wipe you out faster than almost
anything else in investing.
[The stack triples... then a red "MARGIN CALL" alarm flashes.]

--- THE IDEA (≈0:12–0:35) ---

Here's how it works. Your broker lends you money to invest, on top of your own
cash. You put in 1,000, borrow another 2,000 — and now you control 3,000 dollars
of stock. That's leverage.
[The position scaled up 3×.]
If that stock rises 10%, you don't make 10% on your thousand. You make 10% on
three thousand — three times the gain.
[+10% on $3,000 = +$300, vs +$100 unleveraged.]

--- THE CATCH (≈0:35–1:05) ---

But it works exactly the same way in reverse.
[The chart turns red.]
A 10% drop is now a 10% loss on three thousand dollars — three times the hit to
your own money. Leverage cuts both ways, equally.
[−10% on $3,000 = −$300, eating deep into the $1,000.]
And there's a trap. Your investments are the collateral for that loan. If they
fall too far, the broker issues a margin call: add more cash immediately — or they
sell your positions automatically, often at the worst possible moment.
[A "MARGIN CALL" alarm; positions force-sold at the bottom.]
You can walk away owing more than you ever put in.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:05–1:18) ---

So margin doesn't change whether you're right or wrong.
[The same trade, scaled up in both directions.]
It just multiplies the result. It's an accelerator pedal with no brakes — bigger
wins, and brutal losses.
