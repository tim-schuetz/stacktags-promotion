VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: Tether — the most genius business model ever invented
Topic: Investing / finance
Target length: ~2 minutes (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

Style: continuous faux-3D camera over the persistent grey grid (depth transitions
between beats — never hard cuts). White + turquoise (#35A292) only, Inter only, no
borders. Subtitles mirror the narration verbatim (grey, key words turquoise).
Swoosh only when the grid moves or a default element animates.

RECURRING MOTIF (establish once, reuse so scenes chain): the USDT token (clean
turquoise CSS coin, "₮ / USDT"), the $1 bill (cropped photo, dashed outline), the
VAULT (cropped photo of a cash-stuffed safe, dashed outline), the US T-BILL (cropped
Treasury-bill doc, dashed outline). Two cartoon characters (NO dashed outline): a
crypto TRADER and the Tether FOUNDER. Reconstructed news HEADLINE cards (realistic
styling, generic outlets — no real logos) are a motif, reused in the hook and the
"fined before" beat.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Tether issues USDT, a "stablecoin" pegged 1:1 to the US dollar. You give them a
real dollar, they mint you a token, and they KEEP the dollar — which they invest
(mostly in US Treasury bills, ~4-5% yield) while paying token holders 0%. With
100B+ dollars of USDT out there, that's an interest-free, ever-growing pile of
other people's money, and Tether keeps 100% of the return. It's "float" — the
holy grail of finance.
Punchline: if you get to issue the dollar, you get to keep the interest.

NOTE (honesty — keep one short "catch" beat):
- Figures are as REPORTED by Tether (attestations, not full independent audits).
- Reserves are MOSTLY T-bills + other assets — say "mostly."
- Tether has been FINED before (CFTC/NYAG) for overstating reserves.
- The model depends on TRUST: a mass redemption / bank-run is the failure mode.
- "Infinite leverage" is hyperbole → frame as free, growing, interest-free float.
