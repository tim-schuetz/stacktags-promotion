VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: The place where astronauts are your nearest neighbors
Topic: Geography
Target length: ~90 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Point Nemo (South Pacific) is the spot in the ocean farthest from any land —
~2,688 km from the nearest coast in every direction. That's so far that when the
ISS passes overhead (~400 km up), the astronauts are the closest humans to you.
It's also the "spacecraft cemetery," where agencies deorbit dead satellites and
stations (including Mir).
Punchline: the loneliest spot on Earth's surface is where we drop things falling
out of the sky.

KEY VISUALS (as requested):
- A globe zooming onto empty ocean; the ISS orbit drawn passing overhead; a
  seafloor scattered with old spacecraft.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

At the loneliest spot on Earth, your nearest neighbors aren't on the planet at
all. They're in orbit.
[Globe zooms to a blank patch of the South Pacific; an ISS orbit arcs overhead.]
This is Point Nemo — and when the space station flies over, the astronauts on
board are closer to you than any other human being alive.
[A figure on the empty sea; the ISS overhead marked "closest humans."]

--- HOW FAR IS FAR (≈0:12–0:40) ---

Point Nemo is the point in the ocean farthest from any land. In every direction,
the nearest coastline is about 2,688 kilometers away.
[A ring expanding out to three distant specks of land, all ~2,688 km off.]
That's so far that when the International Space Station passes over — orbiting just
about 400 kilometers up —
[A height comparison: 400 km up vs 2,688 km sideways.]
the people directly above you are nearer than anyone on the ground. Your closest
neighbors are in space.

--- THE SPACECRAFT GRAVEYARD (≈0:40–1:05) ---

And because it's so far from everyone, space agencies use it for something else: a
graveyard.
[Re-entry streaks falling toward the empty ocean.]
When a satellite or a space station reaches the end of its life, they steer its
fiery fall down to right here — including the old Russian station, Mir. Hundreds
of dead spacecraft now rest on the seafloor below.
[A seabed littered with the wrecks of old craft.]
One day, the Space Station itself is meant to come down here too.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:05–1:18) ---

So the most isolated place on the surface of the Earth —
[The lone figure, ISS above, wrecks below.]
is also where we send the things falling out of the sky. Down here you're
completely alone... except for the astronauts overhead, and a graveyard of
spaceships beneath you.
