VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: The highest mountain on Earth is NOT Everest
Topic: Geography
Target length: ~100 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Everest is the highest ABOVE SEA LEVEL (~8,849 m). But Earth isn't a perfect
sphere — it bulges ~21 km at the equator (spin / centrifugal effect). Measured
from Earth's CENTER, the winner is Chimborazo in Ecuador (~6,263 m above sea
level, but sitting almost on the equatorial bulge). Its summit is ~6,384 km from
the core vs Everest's ~6,382 km → Chimborazo's peak reaches ~2,168 m FARTHER into
space, making it the point on Earth's surface closest to outer space.
Punchline: "highest" depends where you measure from — from the center, the real
summit is in Ecuador.

NOTE (honesty): keep it fair — Everest is still highest above sea level; Mauna
Kea is tallest base-to-summit. Chimborazo wins ONE specific title (farthest from
Earth's center). Say so.

KEY VISUALS (as requested):
- A globe with an exaggerated EQUATORIAL BULGE.
- A vector drawn from Earth's CORE to each summit; Chimborazo's vector is longer
  and "beats" Everest's.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:10) — the payoff up front ---

The highest mountain on Earth is not Mount Everest.
[A bold title; Everest with a red ✗.]
Measure it the right way, and the winner is a volcano in Ecuador most people have
never heard of: Chimborazo. Its peak juts more than two kilometers farther into
space than Everest's.
[Chimborazo rises; a "winner" tag pops over it.]
Here's how that's even possible.

--- THE CATCH WITH EVEREST (≈0:10–0:32) ---

Everest really is the tallest mountain — above sea level. Nothing on the planet
rises higher above the ocean's surface: about 8,849 meters.
[Everest with a ruler measuring up from sea level.]
But "above sea level" quietly assumes the Earth is a perfect sphere.
[A clean sphere... that then starts to squash.]
And it isn't.

--- WHY THE EARTH IS FAT (≈0:32–0:55) ---

The Earth spins — and that spin flings it outward around the middle. So the whole
planet bulges at the equator.
[GLOBE with an exaggerated equatorial bulge; arrows pushing outward at the middle.]
It's about 21 kilometers fatter at the equator than from pole to pole. The
equator literally sticks out farther from the center of the Earth.
[A "+21 km" marker on the bulge.]
So let's measure mountains a fairer way — not from the sea, but from the very
center of the planet.
[A vector is drawn from Earth's core outward.]

--- CHIMBORAZO WINS (≈0:55–1:25) ---

And this is where Chimborazo wins. It sits almost exactly on the equator — right
on top of that bulge — while Everest is far to the north.
[Two pins: Chimborazo on the equator, Everest up at ~28° north.]
So even though Chimborazo is "only" 6,263 meters above sea level — over two and a
half kilometers shorter than Everest — it's standing on a platform that's already
pushed kilometers farther out into space.
[Chimborazo's base perched high on the bulge; Everest's on the thinner part.]
Run the measurement from the core, and Chimborazo's summit sits about 6,384
kilometers out — Everest's, about 6,382.
[Two vectors from the core; Chimborazo's clearly longer, overtaking Everest's.]
Chimborazo wins by roughly 2,168 meters. Which makes its peak the single closest
point on the Earth's surface to outer space.
[The summit glows, reaching toward the stars.]

--- THE HONEST FOOTNOTE + PUNCHLINE (≈1:25–1:45) ---

Now, to be fair: Everest is still the highest above sea level, and a Hawaiian
volcano, Mauna Kea, is actually the tallest from base to peak.
[Three mountains, three labels: "above sea level," "base to summit," "from Earth's center."]
"Highest" just depends on where you start your ruler.
So next time someone calls Everest the top of the world —
[Everest, then the camera pulls back to the whole spinning globe.]
remember: from the center of the Earth, the real summit is in Ecuador.

--- OUTRO / CTA (≈1:45–1:55) ---
