VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: Japan now sells more adult diapers than baby ones
Topic: Demographics
Target length: ~95 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Japan is aging so fast that adult (incontinence) diapers now outsell baby diapers
— a crossover reached by around the mid-2010s. In 2024, paper-goods maker Oji
announced it would stop producing baby diapers in Japan entirely and shift to
adult diapers, as births collapsed. A demographic warning sign you can hold in
your hand.
Punchline: you can read a country's future on its store shelves.

NOTE (honesty): the exact crossover year varies by source (reports range ~2011 to
mid-2010s) — say "by around the mid-2010s." Oji's exit from domestic baby-diaper
production was announced in 2024.

KEY VISUALS:
- A store aisle: the baby-diaper shelf shrinking, the adult-diaper shelf growing,
  until they cross over.
- A population pyramid inverting (few young, many old).

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

In Japan, this ordinary product quietly tells you the country's entire future:
the diaper.
[A single diaper on a plain background.]
Because somewhere in the last decade, Japan started selling more diapers for
adults than for babies. And recently, one major manufacturer stopped making baby
diapers altogether.
[Two shelves: "BABY" shrinking, "ADULT" swelling, lines crossing.]

--- WHAT THE SHELVES SHOW (≈0:12–0:42) ---

Japan is aging faster than almost any country on Earth — fewer and fewer babies,
more and more elderly. And nowhere is that clearer than the diaper aisle.
[A population pyramid slowly flips top-heavy.]
By around the mid-2010s, sales of adult incontinence diapers had overtaken baby
diapers. And in 2024, Oji — one of Japan's big paper-goods makers — announced it
would stop producing baby diapers in Japan entirely, and turn that line over to
adult ones.
[A factory sign: "BABY DIAPERS" switched off; "ADULT DIAPERS" switched on.]
The baby market had simply shrunk too far to keep going.

--- THE BIGGER PICTURE (≈0:42–1:08) ---

This isn't a quirky retail stat. Japan's population is falling by hundreds of
thousands of people every year.
[A counter ticking downward; a map of Japan slowly fading at the edges.]
More adult diapers than baby diapers is a demographic warning sign you can
literally hold in your hand. And Japan isn't alone — South Korea, Italy, China,
much of the world is aging the same way.
[The same crossover graph appears over several country flags.]
Japan is just there first.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:08–1:20) ---

You can read a nation's future in budgets and charts...
[Spreadsheets and graphs flash by.]
or you can just look at what's on the shelf. In Japan, the shelves already
decided: more diapers for the old than for the young.
