==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Counterintuitive truth: crypto is NOT anonymous — by design, almost every
transaction is public and permanent on the blockchain, so stolen funds are visible
to the whole world. Told through the real case of the Lazarus Group (North Korea)
and the record ~$1.5B exchange hack. Stealing is easy; SPENDING is hard. To break
the trail, the thieves rely on three moves: chain hopping, mixers, and splitting.
Punchline: crypto is the most transparent money ever made — which is exactly why
even a state-backed hacker can't just walk up and spend a stolen billion.

NOTE (responsibility): this is an AWARENESS / EXPLAINER piece on why crypto is
traceable — NOT a how-to. Keep the techniques CONCEPTUAL (what they are, one line
each); no operational steps or service recommendations.

NOTE (accuracy): the user wrote "Bitget"; the record February-2025 Lazarus heist
was BYBIT (~$1.5B), the biggest crypto theft ever. Script uses the Bybit case —
flagged to user.

VISUAL SYSTEM (applies to every scene)

- SOUND: swoosh ONLY when the background grid actually moves OR a default element
