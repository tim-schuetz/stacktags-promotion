==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Counterintuitive truth: crypto is NOT anonymous — by design, almost every
transaction is public and permanent on the blockchain, so stolen funds are visible
to the whole world. WHY: a blockchain is not stored in one place — an identical copy
of the ledger lives on thousands of computers (nodes/servers) worldwide, and every
transaction is broadcast to all of them. Told through the real Lazarus Group (North
Korea) ~$1.5B exchange hack. Stealing is easy; SPENDING is hard. To break the trail
the thieves rely on three moves: chain hopping, mixers, splitting.
Punchline: crypto is the most transparent money ever made — which is exactly why
even a state-backed hacker can't just walk up and spend a stolen billion.

NOTE (responsibility): AWARENESS / EXPLAINER on why crypto is traceable — NOT a
how-to. Techniques stay CONCEPTUAL (one line each); no operational steps.

NOTE (accuracy): the record February-2025 Lazarus heist was BYBIT (~$1.5B), the
biggest crypto theft ever. Script uses the Bybit case.

--- WHY: THE STRUCTURE (≈0:20–0:42) — the new explanation ---

And it comes down to every blockchain's most innate structure.
[Depth zoom-OUT (grid converges → swoosh) hands off to SCENE sc-network — the

The ledger isn't kept in one place. An identical copy of it lives on thousands of

[All the servers are now present and connected; each clearly holds the SAME little

Every single transaction is broadcast to all of them, and locked into the very same

[On "broadcast", coins (₿) start gliding along the lines BETWEEN the servers in both

[SCENE sc-enum — `enumeration_with_in_detail_follow_up` default element. Border-less
