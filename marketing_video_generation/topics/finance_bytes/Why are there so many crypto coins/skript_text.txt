==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
There are now millions of crypto coins because each wave fixed a limit of the last
AND made minting new coins easier:
1. Bitcoin — great money, but slow and can basically only do one thing.
2. Ethereum — added "smart contracts," so blockchains could run apps; anyone could
   create a token in a few lines of code.
3. Rival chains (Solana, BNB, etc.) — launched to be faster/cheaper than a
   congested Ethereum; each brings its own coin.
4. Memecoin explosion — platforms like Pump.fun let anyone mint a token in seconds
   for almost nothing → millions of joke/speculative coins.
Punchline: crypto kept fixing its own limits, and each fix made it easier to spit
out the next coin.

NOTE (honesty): the vast majority of these coins have no real use and are highly
speculative or outright scams — quantity isn't quality.

KEY VISUALS:
- A timeline/tree: 1 Bitcoin → Ethereum branching into many tokens → rival chains →
  an explosion of memecoins flooding the screen.

  information or a beat of humour. Coins/chains are rendered as ON-BRAND turquoise
  glyph-chips (₿ Ξ SOL BNB), NOT their real multi-colour logos (keeps white+turquoise).
- IMAGES (Gemini → rembg cut-out): a single real ₿ Bitcoin coin (dashed silhouette
  outline, it's a single object). The memecoin faces (dog / sunglasses-celebrity /

  it stays crisp and on-brand (the Pump.fun launch card is REBUILT as a clean UI, like

- SOUND: ticking while numbers count up (the millions counter, the rising gas fee);
  pop when a token/word springs in; swoosh only when the grid actually moves or a

- DEFAULT ELEMENTS reused: text-popup (springy key terms), the depth-transition grid,
  outro (closing brand card). A counting headline (ticking) for the hook & the fee.

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

There are now millions of different cryptocurrencies. Not thousands — millions.
[SCENE 1 — THE COUNTER. Enter on a zoom-in. A huge tabular-number headline counts up
fast from 0, ticking SFX rising in pitch; it blows past a struck-through "thousands"
and locks onto "1,000,000+" in deep turquoise. As it crosses into the millions, dozens
of small turquoise coin-chips (₿ Ξ ¢ generic) flood IN from all four screen edges and
pile up behind the number. Sub keyword "millions" turquoise. Busy, overwhelming — on
purpose.]
And it all started because the very first one, Bitcoin, was simply too slow.
[SCENE 2 — depth zoom-OUT: the coin-flood recedes and we land on ONE real ₿ Bitcoin
coin (assets/bitcoin.png — Gemini photo, cut out, dashed silhouette outline). It inches
slowly along a single grid line at a tired, sluggish pace (a tiny motion-trail behind
it). Subtitle keyword "too slow" turquoise. Swoosh on the camera move.]

--- WAVE 1: BITCOIN (≈0:12–0:32) ---

Bitcoin was the original — brilliant as digital money. But it's limited. It's slow,
and it can basically do just one thing: move bitcoin.
[SCENE 3 — the ₿ coin docks onto a SINGLE clean turquoise rail/track that runs left→
right. One coin slides A→B along the one rail (its one job). A text-popup chip "move
BTC — that's it." springs in (pop). Minimal: one coin, one rail, nothing else.]
You can't really build anything on top of it. So people wanted more.
[SCENE 4 — (depth transition, no cut) try to STACK on top: 2–3 turquoise app-blocks
drop onto the coin/rail but slide off and topple with a springy bounce; a small grey
"?" puff. The single rail can't hold a tower. Keyword "build" greyed.]

--- WAVE 2: ETHEREUM (≈0:32–0:55) ---

That's where Ethereum came in. It added "smart contracts" — little programs that
run on the blockchain itself.
[SCENE 5 — depth DROP to Ethereum: a wide turquoise PLATFORM slab labelled Ξ Ethereum
slides up from the bottom; small rounded "app-blocks" (each a tiny icon: a swap, a
game pad, a picture) pop up and sit RUNNING on top, gently pulsing. A soft chip label
"smart contracts" appears. This is the thing Bitcoin couldn't do.]
Suddenly a blockchain could host apps, not just payments. And crucially, anyone
could create their own token on top of Ethereum with just a few lines of code.
[SCENE 6 — a small monospace CODE-SNIPPET card slides in (≈4 lines of an ERC-20-style
"mint" stub, syntax-tinted turquoise). A cursor hits a "Deploy" line → a fresh token-
chip POPS out of the card (pop). Mirrors the good "code → object" example: the visual
teaches that a token is just a few lines of code.]
The floodgates cracked open.
[SCENE 7 — that one token becomes several, then MANY: a rapid cascade of token-chips
multiplying out of the card and spreading across the platform (quick stutter of pops +
a swoosh as the grid pushes back). Keyword "floodgates" turquoise.]

--- WAVE 3: THE RIVALS (≈0:55–1:18) ---

Then Ethereum got so popular it became slow and expensive to use.
[SCENE 8 — the Ethereum platform now CONGESTED: the app-blocks are jammed shoulder-to-
shoulder and jitter in place; a "GAS FEE $" readout ticks UP fast (ticking SFX) while a
little clock-spinner says slow. Nothing red — congestion shown by density + the rising
number. Keywords "slow and expensive" turquoise.]
So a wave of rival blockchains launched, each promising to be faster or cheaper —
Solana, BNB, and many more. And every single one comes with its own coin.
[SCENE 9 — depth pan to a cluster of RIVAL CHAINS as on-brand turquoise node-chips:
SOL, BNB, AVAX, … each pops in (pop/swoosh) with a micro-label "faster · cheaper", and
each chip drops its OWN little coin beneath it. They keep popping past the frame edge to
say "and many more". NOT real brand colours — stays white+turquoise.]

--- WAVE 4: THE MEMECOIN FLOOD (≈1:18–1:40) ---

And finally, it became trivial to make a coin at all. Platforms like Pump.fun let

[SCENE 10 — a REBUILT "launch a coin" UI card (Pump.fun-style, clean & on-brand): a

Recreating the interface = research/value, not a literal screenshot.]
The result: a flood of memecoins — coins based on jokes, dogs, celebrities — most
with no purpose beyond speculation, and most worthless within days.
[SCENE 11 — a TIDAL FLOOD of joke coins pours down the screen (rapid pops). Riding the
wave: 2–3 cartoon MEMECOIN FACES — a goofy dog coin (assets/coin-dog.png), a sunglasses
"celebrity" coin (assets/coin-celeb.png), a rocket/moon coin (assets/coin-moon.png),
Gemini cartoons, NO dashed outline. On "worthless within days" the whole wave drains of
colour to faint grey and slides/falls to the bottom — a value line crashing to zero
(downward swoosh). The only place we let it get silly = the punctual humour.]

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:40–1:54) ---

So why are there millions of coins? Because crypto kept fixing its own
limitations —
[SCENE 12 — THE TREE. A clean vertical branch-tree assembles top→down, one branch per
