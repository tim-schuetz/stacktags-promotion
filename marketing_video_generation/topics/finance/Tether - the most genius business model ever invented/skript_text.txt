==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Tether issues USDT, a "stablecoin" pegged 1:1 to the US dollar. You give them a
real dollar, they mint you a token, and they KEEP the dollar — which they invest
(mostly in US Treasury bills, ~4-5% yield) while paying token holders 0%. With
100B+ dollars of USDT out there, that's an interest-free, ever-growing pile of
other people's money, and Tether keeps 100% of the return. It's "float" — the
holy grail of finance.
Punchline: if you get to issue the dollar, you get to keep the interest.

NOTE (honesty — keep one short "catch" beat):
- Figures are as REPORTED by Tether (attestations, not full independent audits).
- Reserves are MOSTLY T-bills + other assets — say "mostly."
- Tether has been FINED before (CFTC/NYAG) for overstating reserves.
- The model depends on TRUST: a mass redemption / bank-run is the failure mode.
- "Infinite leverage" is hyperbole → frame as free, growing, interest-free float.

SCRIPT

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

There's a company that makes more money per employee than almost anyone on Earth.
[A tiny office; an enormous pile of cash stacked behind it.]
Around a hundred staff. Billions in annual profit. And the entire business model
is this: you give them a dollar, they hand you a token — and they keep the dollar.
[$1 slides in → a "USDT" token slides out → the dollar drops into Tether's vault.]
That's Tether. And it's completely legal. Here's why it might be the most genius
business model ever invented.

--- WHAT IT ACTUALLY IS (≈0:12–0:35) ---

Tether runs USDT — a "stablecoin." One USDT is always meant to equal one US dollar.
[1 USDT ⇄ $1, locked together.]
Crypto traders love it: it's a dollar that lives on the blockchain, so they can
jump in and out of trades instantly. To get one, you hand Tether a real dollar,
and they mint you a token.
[A trader swaps $1 for 1 USDT.]
Simple. But the magic isn't the token — it's what happens to your dollar.

--- THE TRICK (≈0:35–1:00) ---

Because Tether doesn't just let your dollar sit in a drawer. It takes all those
billions and parks them mostly in US Treasury bills — basically the safest loan
on the planet — earning around 4 to 5% a year.
[The vault of dollars flows into "US T-BILLS"; a yield meter ticks up to ~5%.]
And how much of that interest do they pass back to you, the token holder?
[A big fat 0% stamps onto the screen.]
Zero. You're holding a dollar that pays you nothing — and Tether keeps every cent
of the yield.

--- THE SCALE: FREE MONEY (≈1:00–1:25) ---

Now multiply it. There's well over a hundred billion dollars of USDT out there —
which means well over a hundred billion of other people's money, working for
Tether, for free.
[A counter spins past $100,000,000,000.]
And normally, getting capital is expensive: a bank pays interest to its
depositors, a company pays interest on its bonds. Everyone pays for money.
[Banks and companies handing out interest payments.]
Tether's cost? Nothing. It's an interest-free loan from millions of users — and
it keeps 100% of the return. In 2024, the company reported around 13 billion
dollars in profit, with a team you could fit in a single room.

--- THE CATCH (≈1:25–1:48) ---

So what's the catch? Trust.
[The token balanced on the very top of the vault.]
The whole thing holds up only as long as people believe every token is really
backed by a real dollar — and don't all rush to cash out at once. Tether has been
fined before for overstating what was actually in its reserves, and it publishes
"attestations," not full independent audits.
[A magnifying glass moves over a reserve report; a "?" hovers.]
The model is brilliant. But it runs entirely on confidence.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:48–2:02) ---

Still — it cracked something most people never even see. The most profitable
trade in finance wasn't picking the right stock, or the right coin.
[Stock charts and coin logos fade away.]
It was realizing that if you get to issue the dollar... you get to keep the
interest.
[The USDT token glows on a throne built of T-bills.]

Want more business models that sound illegal but aren't?
