VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: Learn 10 Chinese words in 100 seconds
Topic: Learn Chinese
Target length: ~100 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Voiceover script. Spoken lines are plain text. [Bracketed lines] describe
what is on screen AT THE SAME TIME as the narration next to them.

==================================================================
THE 10 CHARACTERS (all are pictographs — the drawing IS the meaning)
==================================================================
1. 人  rén   — person
2. 火  huǒ   — fire
3. 水  shuǐ  — water
4. 山  shān  — mountain
5. 木  mù    — tree / wood
6. 日  rì    — sun / day
7. 月  yuè   — moon / month
8. 口  kǒu   — mouth
9. 雨  yǔ    — rain
10. 田 tián  — field

Recurring visual device — "the morph":
A real photo of the thing morphs into its Chinese character, and the matching
strokes light up. Used for every word.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:10) — the payoff lands in the first 5 seconds ---

In the next 100 seconds you'll learn 10 Chinese characters —
[The 10 characters pop into a quick grid.] without memorizing a single one.

Most people think Chinese is impossible:
[Screen floods with a wall of complicated characters scrolling past.]
thousands of characters that look like random scribbles. But the oldest, most
common ones aren't random —
[The wall fades to just a few simple characters.]
they're tiny drawings of what they mean.
[MORPH: a real flame → 火, a real mountain → 山.]
See it once, and you can't unsee it. Watch.

[ENUMERATION ANIMATION: the 10 characters line up 1–10, camera zooms into number 1.]

--- THE 10 (≈0:10–1:15, about 5 seconds each) ---

Start with you.
1) 人 — "person."
[MORPH: a walking person → 人; the two legs light up.]
A body and two legs, mid-stride. One human, two strokes.

Give them a fire to gather around.
2) 火 — "fire."
[MORPH: a campfire → 火; flame first, then the two sparks.]
A flame in the middle, two sparks flying off the sides. A campfire.

3) 水 — "water."
[MORPH: a stream → 水; current first, then droplets each side.]
A stream down the middle, droplets splashing off both sides. A river in three strokes.

4) 山 — "mountain."
[MORPH: a mountain range → 山; tall peak, then the two shorter ones.]
Three peaks — one tall, two short. The mountain a kid would draw...

5) 木 — "tree" or "wood."
[MORPH: a tree → 木; trunk, branches up, roots down.]
Trunk, branches reaching up, roots spreading down...

6) 日 — "sun," and also "day."
[MORPH: the sun's disc → a circle with a dot → 日.]
A circle with a dot, later squared off to write it faster. The sun marks a day —
so it also means "day."

Next is
7) 月 — "moon," and also "month."
[MORPH: a crescent moon → 月.]
A crescent on its side. One moon cycle is a month — so it means "month" too.

8) 口 — "mouth."
[MORPH: an open mouth → 口.]
An open mouth — just a square opening. The hole you eat and talk with.

9) 雨 — "rain."
[MORPH: a cloud raining → 雨; sky line, cloud box, four drops inside.]
Sky on top, a cloud, four raindrops falling inside. Rain, drawn out.

And it has to land somewhere.
10) 田 — "field."
[MORPH: farmland from above → 田; square, then the cross splitting it in four.]
Farmland from above, split into four plots. A rice field — exactly like one.

--- THE TWIST / BONUS PAYOFF (≈1:15–1:38) ---

Now the part that makes Chinese click: stack the pictures and they build new words.
[The 10 characters slide to the edges, ready to combine in the center.]

Two trees —
[木 + 木 → 林, then a third → 森.]
木 plus 木 makes 林, a wood. Add a third and 森 is a dense forest.

Sun plus moon —
[日 + 月 → 明, glowing.]
日 plus 月 makes 明, "bright." The two brightest things in the sky.

A person by a tree —
[人 + 木 → 休.]
人 plus 木 makes 休, "rest."

And one extra for free: 力 means "strength."
[田 + 力 → 男.]
Strength in a field — 田 plus 力 — makes 男, "man," the one who works the field.

--- OUTRO / CTA (≈1:38–1:48) ---

That's the secret: Chinese isn't random symbols — it's a small set of pictures,
combined in ways that make sense.
[The characters swirl back into one clean grid.]

You just read 10 pictures. No memorizing...
