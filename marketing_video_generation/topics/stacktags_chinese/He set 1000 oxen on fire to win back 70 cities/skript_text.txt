==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The "fire-ox formation" (火牛阵), ~279 BCE. The state of Qi was crushed by Yan, down to
its last two cities. Defending general Tian Dan strapped blades to the horns of ~1,000
oxen, painted them as monstrous dragons, tied burning reeds to their tails, and drove
them at night through holes in the walls into the enemy camp — followed by 5,000
soldiers. The panicked Yan army shattered, and Qi reconquered all ~70 lost cities.
Punchline: a war turned around by a stampede of flaming, blade-horned cattle.

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — the payoff up front ---

A Chinese general was losing a war so badly he had one city left. So he tied knives to
the horns of a thousand oxen, set their tails on fire, and stampeded them at the enemy
in the dark. By morning, he'd won back seventy cities.

--- THE DESPERATE SITUATION (≈0:16–0:42) ---

Around 279 BC, the state of Qi was almost wiped off the map. Its rival, Yan, had
overrun the entire country — seventy-some cities gone — until Qi was cornered into its
final two strongholds. Inside one of them was a clever commander named Tian Dan, with a
starving population and no way to win a normal fight. So he stopped planning a normal
fight.

--- THE FIRE-OX PLAN (≈0:42–1:14) ---

He gathered around a thousand oxen from the city. To their horns, he lashed sharp
blades. Across their bodies, he draped red silk painted with monstrous dragon patterns,
to terrify anyone who saw them. And to their tails, he tied bundles of reeds soaked in
oil. Then, in the dead of night, he opened secret holes cut in the city walls — lit the
reeds — and the oxen, suddenly burning at the rear, went mad. A thousand panicked,
flaming, blade-horned animals exploded out of the walls and straight into the sleeping
Yan camp. Behind them charged five thousand of Tian Dan's best soldiers, faces painted,
screaming. The Yan troops woke to what looked like fire-breathing dragons goring and
trampling everything in the dark.

--- THE COMEBACK (≈1:14–1:30) ---

They broke and ran. Their commander was killed in the chaos. And that single night
flipped the entire war. Riding the momentum, Tian Dan chased the shattered army out of
Qi — and one after another, took back all seventy lost cities. A nation, resurrected
from one city, by a herd of cattle.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:30–1:42) ---

It's still studied as one of the greatest comebacks in military history. Proof that
when you can't win with strength, you can sometimes win with sheer, flaming imagination.
