VIDEO SCRIPT
Title options (pick one): The chopstick mistake that means death in China
Topic: Learn Chinese
Target length: ~85 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Spoken voiceover narration + [bracketed] visual cues. The brackets are NOT spoken —
they are stripped before TTS; the spoken text is exactly the un-bracketed words.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Sticking chopsticks straight up in a bowl of rice is a deep taboo: it mirrors the
incense/rice offerings made to the dead at funerals and for ancestors. So it reads as
an omen of death at the table. (Death-related taboos intensify during the 7th lunar
month — "Ghost Month.") Etiquette is really old belief, frozen into manners.
Punchline: stand your chopsticks up, and you've quietly turned dinner into a funeral.

VISUAL SPINE (anti-slop): ONE recurring hero object — the same white rice bowl — carries the
whole video; scenes are chained transitions of that object, not a new scene per sentence.
The clever beat: the upright chopsticks visually RHYME with the upright incense sticks of a
funeral offering (that IS the cultural reason). Tone = subtle & symbolic (smoke, a white
chrysanthemum — never a literal altar/portrait). A small comic diner adds light humor.
On-screen text only when it ADDS info: the vocab 筷子 (kuàizi) and 鬼月 (guǐyuè). White grid bg,
white + turquoise only. Swoosh SFX only when the grid moves / a default element animates.

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

[HERO bowl of white rice, centered on the white grid. Bottom subtitle only — no explanatory on-screen text.]
There's one thing you must never do with chopsticks in China: stand them straight up
in your bowl of rice.
[On "stand them straight up": the two chopsticks SLAM upright into the rice (springy pop).
Vocab 筷子 (kuàizi) pops in briefly via text-popup, then fades — the only on-screen word.]
Do it, and you've just set a place... for the dead.
[On "for the dead": a single thin wisp of incense-smoke curls up behind the bowl; a slow ominous
camera push-in (the grid converges).]
[Slow ominous camera push-in — the grid converges (zoom-in), bowl recedes slightly. Swoosh.]

--- WHY IT'S TABOO (≈0:12–0:42) ---

It's one of the deepest dining taboos in Chinese culture.
[Faux-3D depth transition (grid moves → swoosh). The dinner bowl with upright chopsticks holds
on the LEFT.]
And the reason is what it resembles.
[On "what it resembles": a censer with two upright incense sticks + rising smoke and a single
white chrysanthemum SLIDES IN from the right (dashed-outline cutouts). Same silhouette, same
upright posture as the chopsticks — laid out as a mirrored pair.]
At funerals and ancestor rituals, a bowl of rice with incense — or chopsticks —
standing straight up is offered to the deceased. It's a gift for the dead.
[A turquoise dashed line connects the two upright pairs (chopsticks ↔ incense), spelling out the
visual rhyme. AND a desaturated/greyed photo of a REAL Chinese memorial ceremony (altar with
upright incense in a censer, white rice bowls, chrysanthemums, mourners bowing) rises in along the
BOTTOM edge; the bowl + censer slide up a little to make room — grounding the rhyme in the real thing.]
So at a normal dinner, chopsticks jammed upright look exactly like a death offering.
[Camera returns to the dinner bowl; for a beat the upright chopsticks cross-fade toward the
incense look and back — they read as the SAME thing.]
It's read as an omen — almost like inviting death to the table.
[The faint empty place-setting / a pale ghost-wisp drifts at the table edge. Subtle.]

--- THE RIGHT WAY AND GHOST MONTH (≈0:42–1:05) ---

Instead, you lay your chopsticks flat — across the top of the bowl, or on a little
rest.
[The SAME hero bowl. On "lay your chopsticks flat": the two chopsticks rotate in one clean,
satisfying motion from vertical → resting flat across the rim (settle ease). Calm turquoise tint;
smoke clears. No checkmark text.]
Tiny gesture, completely different meaning.
And these death-related taboos get taken even more seriously during the seventh lunar
month — "Ghost Month" —
[On "Ghost Month": background cools toward night (grid dims); a paper lantern rises and glows.
Vocab 鬼月 (guǐyuè) pops in via text-popup — second and last on-screen word.]
when spirits are said to roam, and people avoid anything that might invite bad luck.
[One or two pale wisps drift through; lantern sways. Kept restrained.]

--- PUNCHLINE / LOOP (≈0:48–0:55) ---

It's just two sticks and a bowl of rice.
[Strip back to the bare hero: bowl + two chopsticks resting flat on the clean white grid. Quiet.]
But stand them up, and you've turned dinner into a funeral — which is exactly why
[On "stand them up": the chopsticks snap upright again and smoke rises (the funeral state). The
video ENDS here on the upright chopsticks — NO outro, no brand card — so on a YouTube replay it
loops straight back into the first line: "…which is exactly why → there's one thing you must
never do with chopsticks in China."]
