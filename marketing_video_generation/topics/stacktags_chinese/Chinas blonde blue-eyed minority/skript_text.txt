THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Among China's 56 official ethnic groups is the "Russian" minority (~15,000–16,000),
mostly descendants of Russians — many White Russians who fled before/after the 1917
Revolution. In Enhe and Shiwei (Inner Mongolia / NE China) live blonde, blue-eyed
Chinese citizens who speak fluent northern Chinese.
Punchline: fully Chinese citizens who look Russian and sound Mandarin.

NOTE (honesty): population ~15,000; "number 56" = one of the 56 officially recognized
nationalities (the count, not a rank).

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

China officially recognizes 56 ethnic groups. And one of them is blonde and
blue-eyed. They're Chinese citizens, born and raised, who look like they walked
straight out of Moscow — and speak perfect Mandarin.

--- WHO THEY ARE (≈0:12–0:38) ---

They're known simply as the Russian ethnic minority — one of the smallest of China's
56 recognized nationalities, only around fifteen thousand people. Most are descendants
of Russians who crossed the border generations ago — many of them White Russians,
fleeing the chaos of the 1917 Revolution and the civil war that followed. They settled
in the far north, married locally, stayed — and over a century, became Chinese.

--- WHERE TO FIND THEM (≈0:38–1:02) ---

Today, in border towns like Enhe and Shiwei, up in Inner Mongolia near the Russian
frontier, you'll find villages of log cabins and onion-dome churches — and people with
fair hair and light eyes who are, by every official measure, Chinese. They carry
Chinese ID cards. They went to Chinese schools. Many speak fluent northern Chinese as
their first language — and sometimes barely any Russian at all. A grandmother who
looks Slavic, chatting away in Mandarin.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:02–1:16) ---

It's a perfect reminder that "Chinese" was never about one face. A nation of 1.4
billion has room for blonde hair and blue eyes — citizen number fifty-six, hiding in
plain sight on the northern border.
