THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The Ming military manual Huolongjing describes the "fire dragon out of the water"
(火龙出水): an early TWO-STAGE rocket. Booster rockets carried a ~1.5 m dragon-shaped
body skimming over the water; when the boosters burned out, they automatically ignited
a swarm of smaller arrow-rockets fired from the dragon's mouth at enemy ships. The same
core principle as a modern multistage rocket — ~600 years before NASA.
Punchline: the Saturn V's basic idea, shaped like a dragon, in the 1300s–1400s.

NOTE (honesty): the Huolongjing was compiled ~14th–15th c. (dating/attribution debated);
say "around 600 years" and "described in" the manual.

--- HOOK (0:00–0:14) — the payoff up front ---

Around 600 years before NASA, China designed a two-stage rocket — the same basic idea
that would one day launch humans to the Moon. And it was shaped like a dragon, firing
smaller rockets out of its mouth.

--- THE PRINCIPLE (≈0:14–0:36) ---

Here's the genius of a multistage rocket. One set of engines burns to get you going,
then drops away or hands off — and a second stage takes over to go further. That
hand-off is the secret behind every rocket that's ever reached space. And it's exactly
what's described in an old Chinese military manual called the Huolongjing — the "Fire
Dragon Manual."

--- THE FIRE DRAGON (≈0:36–1:08) ---

The weapon was called the "fire dragon out of the water." Picture a tube about a metre
and a half long, carved and painted as a dragon. Strapped to its body were several
large rockets — the boosters. To attack an enemy fleet, soldiers would light those
boosters, and the dragon would streak low across the surface of the water toward the
ships, skimming over the waves. Then comes the clever part. When those booster rockets
burned out, their fuse automatically lit a second set — a swarm of smaller arrow-
rockets packed inside the dragon's body. And those came shooting out of the dragon's
open mouth, raining fire onto the enemy ships from close range. Stage one carries it to
the target. Stage two does the damage. That's a two-stage rocket.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:08–1:24) ---

The exact same principle that, centuries later, would send rockets into orbit — sketched
out in a Chinese manual in the age of swords and sails. The space age, in dragon form,
600 years early.
