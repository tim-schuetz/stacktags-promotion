Title: China is forgetting how to write
Topic: Learn Chinese
Target length: ~95 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
"Character amnesia" — 提笔忘字 (tí bǐ wàng zì, "pick up the pen, forget the
character"). Because people type via Pinyin (type the sound → pick the character
from a list), they only RECOGNIZE characters, never actively recall them by hand —
so handwriting atrophies, even for common characters. Recognition stays; recall
fades.
Punchline: for millennia, knowing a character meant being able to write it — now a
billion people know their characters but can't quite draw them.

NOTE (honesty): it's a real, studied effect — but it's HANDWRITING that fades;
reading/typing stay fluent. Don't overstate it as "forgetting the language."

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:12) — the payoff up front ---

A billion people can read their own language perfectly — but more and more of them
can't write it by hand anymore. There's even a name for it: character amnesia. And
the cause is sitting in your pocket.

--- THE PHENOMENON (≈0:12–0:38) ---

Picture an educated Chinese speaker, pen in hand — and they suddenly blank on how to
write a character they use every single day. The Chinese term for it is 提笔忘字 —
literally, "pick up the pen, forget the character." And it's spreading. So what's
going on?

--- WHY IT HAPPENS (≈0:38–1:05) ---

It comes down to how you type Chinese... 
You don't draw the characters. You type the
sound. The Pinyin. and your phone shows you a list of matching characters. You
just pick the right one. So all day long, you only have to recognize the correct
character. Never build it from memory, stroke by stroke. And recognition is easy.
Active recall — producing the whole thing from scratch — is hard. Classic
use-it-or-lose-it. People read fluently, type fluently — then freeze the moment they
have to handwrite. Even simple, everyday characters slip away.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:05–1:20) ---

For thousands of years, to truly know a character was to be able to write it. Now, a
billion people know their characters perfectly — and just can't quite draw them
anymore. Maybe the first writing system in history quietly handed over to a keyboard...
