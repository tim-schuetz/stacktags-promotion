--- HOOK (0:00–0:14) — the payoff up front ---

This man was made emperor of China at two years old — worshipped as the Son of Heaven,
ruler of a fifth of humanity. He died decades later as an ordinary gardener, watering
plants in a public park. One life, both of those.

--- THE CHILD EMPEROR (≈0:14–0:38) ---

His name was Puyi. In 1908, at just two years old, he was placed on the throne of the
Qing dynasty — a tiny boy crying on a giant golden seat, while grown men kowtowed to
the ground before him. He was the absolute monarch of China. He was also a toddler. But
the empire around him was already collapsing. And in 1912, when he was six, a revolution
forced him to abdicate — ending not just his reign, but more than two thousand years of
emperors. He stayed living inside the Forbidden City for years, a pretend emperor of
nothing, until he was finally thrown out.

--- THE FALL (≈0:38–1:08) ---

Then his life turned stranger still. The Japanese, invading China, scooped him up and
made him their puppet "emperor" of a fake state in the northeast, called Manchukuo. He
wore the robes again — but he was a prisoner in a golden cage, a figurehead for an
occupying army. When Japan lost the war, he was captured, held by the Soviets, then
handed to China's new Communist government. And there, the former Son of Heaven was put
through years of prison and "re-education" — taught to tie his own shoes, do his own
laundry, live like everyone else.

--- THE GARDENER (≈1:08–1:28) ---

When he was finally released, the man who had once owned all of China was given a job —
as a gardener and a clerk at the Beijing Botanical Garden. The last emperor, on a
modest salary, tending flowers for the public. He even reportedly bought a ticket once
to visit the Forbidden City — the palace that had been his home, now a museum he had to
pay to enter.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:28–1:42) ---

Puyi died in 1967, an ordinary citizen. From a god on a throne, to a man pruning hedges
for a wage — maybe the most extreme fall from power in all of human history.
