VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: He lost both feet for telling the truth about a rock — then it became China's most legendary jewel
Topic: China / history
Target length: ~95 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Spoken voiceover narration only — no on-screen/visual cues.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Why jade is so revered, told through Bian He: he found a raw jade stone, brought it to
the King of Chu; the court jewelers called it a worthless rock → his left foot was cut
off. He tried again under the next king → his right foot was cut off. The third king
finally had the stone cut open: flawless jade. It became the legendary "He Shi Bi" —
later the prize in the famous tale 完璧归赵 and basis of the imperial seal.
Punchline: China's most legendary jewel cost one honest man both his feet.

NOTE (honesty): legendary tale from the Han Feizi tradition; present as the classic
story behind the He Shi Bi.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — the payoff up front ---

A man found a rock. He swore it held priceless jade inside. The experts said he was
lying — and for that, they cut off his left foot. He tried again, and they cut off his
right. He was right all along. And that stone became the most legendary jewel in
Chinese history.

--- THE FIRST KING (≈0:16–0:44) ---

His name was Bian He. Long ago, in the state of Chu, he discovered an unremarkable
chunk of stone — but he was certain it was raw, uncut jade, the most precious thing in
the world. So he carried it to the king as a gift. The royal jewelers took one look,
saw a plain rock, and declared it a fake. The king, furious at being "tricked," had
Bian He punished as a liar — his left foot was chopped off.

--- THE SECOND KING (≈0:44–1:06) ---

Years passed; that king died, and his son took the throne. Bian He, still believing,
brought the same stone again. Again, the jewelers called it worthless. Again, he was
branded a liar — and this time they took his right foot. Now crippled, with nothing
left, Bian He sat at the foot of a mountain and wept over his stone for three days and
nights, until he was crying blood.

--- THE THIRD KING (≈1:06–1:28) ---

Word reached the next king, who was puzzled. Plenty of people lose a foot, he said —
why weep like this? Bian He's answer: he wasn't crying for his feet. He was crying that
a priceless treasure was being called a stone, and an honest man called a liar. So the
king finally ordered the rock cut open. Inside was flawless, perfect jade. They named
it after him — the He Shi Bi.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:28–1:42) ---

That jewel went on to become the stuff of legend — fought over by kings, later carved,
by some accounts, into the imperial seal of China itself. The most treasured jade in
the country — and it cost one stubbornly honest man both his feet to prove it was real.
