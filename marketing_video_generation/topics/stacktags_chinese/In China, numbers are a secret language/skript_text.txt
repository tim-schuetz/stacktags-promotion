==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Chinese number homophones became a slang code. 520 (wǔ-èr-líng) ≈ 我爱你 "I love
you" (→ May 20 is an online romance day); 1314 ≈ "forever" (一生一世); 250 (èrbǎiwǔ)
= "idiot/fool"; 88 ≈ "bye-bye"; 666 = "awesome/skilled." Same trick, opposite
meanings — love letters and insults made of digits.
Punchline: a string of numbers in a Chinese chat could be a maths problem… a love
letter… or an insult.

NOTE (honesty): 250 = "fool" — etymology is debated (the "half a string of coins" /
半吊子 story) → say "the story goes."
