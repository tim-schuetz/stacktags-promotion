==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The Nuosu (a branch of the Yi, in the Liangshan mountains) lived under a rigid caste
system into the 1950s: "Black Bone" (Nuoho) aristocracy on top, "White Bone" serfs/
commoners below, and beneath them hereditary slaves (often captured Han). Slavery was
abolished only by the "Democratic Reforms" of 1956–58.
Punchline: a full slave society survived in China into living memory.

NOTE (honesty): "black/white bones" = the caste metaphor (Nuoho/Qunuo); slavery
abolished ~1956–58 in Liangshan. Keep factual.

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:14) — the payoff up front ---

As late as the 1950s — within living memory — a corner of China was still running a
full slave society. And your entire fate was decided by something they called the
color of your bones.

--- THE PEOPLE (≈0:14–0:34) ---

Deep in the Liangshan mountains of southwest China lived the Nuosu, a branch of the Yi
people. And their society was split into rigid castes you were born into and could
never leave. The line between them wasn't money, or skin — it was, in their words,
your bones.

--- BLACK AND WHITE BONES (≈0:34–1:04) ---

At the top were the "Black Bone" — the aristocracy. A proud warrior nobility who owned
the land and ruled everyone below. Beneath them were the "White Bone" — commoners and
serfs, who worked and owed duties to their lords. And at the very bottom were the
slaves. Many were captured outsiders — often Han Chinese, seized in raids on the
lowlands — and their children were born into slavery too, the property of a master for
life. Bought, sold, and inherited like livestock. This wasn't ancient history. It was
the living, functioning structure of an entire society in the twentieth century.

--- THE END (≈1:04–1:22) ---

It only ended when the new Chinese state pushed into the mountains and forced through
what it called the "Democratic Reforms," around 1956 to 1958 — abolishing the caste
system and freeing the slaves, sometimes overnight. People who had been born property
suddenly became citizens.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:22–1:34) ---

We tend to file slavery away as something from the distant past. But in the mountains
of China, a society of masters and slaves — of black bones and white — survived right
up until the age of the television.
