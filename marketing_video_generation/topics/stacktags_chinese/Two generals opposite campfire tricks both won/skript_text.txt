==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Two famous deceptions using cooking-fire counts. Sun Bin REDUCED his army's stoves each
day (减灶) to fake mass desertion and lure overconfident Pang Juan into an ambush at
Maling. Centuries later, Yu Xu INCREASED his stoves each day (增灶) to fake growing
reinforcements and scare off pursuers. Opposite moves, same trick: control what the
enemy thinks your numbers are.
Punchline: in war the truth doesn't matter — only what the enemy counts.

--- HOOK (0:00–0:14) — the payoff up front ---

Two Chinese generals, centuries apart, fooled their enemies with the exact same thing:
campfires. But one did it by lighting fewer fires every day — and the other by lighting
more. Both were brilliant. Here's why.

--- HOW YOU COUNT AN ARMY (≈0:14–0:36) ---

In ancient war, you couldn't see inside the enemy camp. So how did you guess how many
soldiers they had? You counted their cooking fires. Each morning, scouts would tally
the stoves left behind — so many fires meant so many men. Which means: if you control
the number of fires the enemy sees, you control the number of soldiers they think
you have.

--- SUN BIN: FEWER FIRES (≈0:36–1:04) ---

First, the master, Sun Bin. He was up against an arrogant rival general, Pang Juan, who
thought Sun Bin's troops were cowards. So Sun Bin fed that belief. As his army marched,
he had them build fewer and fewer cooking stoves each day — a hundred thousand fires,
then half that, then half again. To Pang Juan's scouts, it looked like Sun Bin's army
was melting away, soldiers deserting in droves. Convinced his enemy was collapsing,
Pang Juan dropped his guard, raced ahead with a light force to finish them off — and
charged straight into Sun Bin's ambush. It was a slaughter.

--- YU XU: MORE FIRES (≈1:04–1:24) ---

Centuries later, a general named Yu Xu faced the opposite problem: he was outnumbered
and being chased. So he flipped the trick. Each day, he had his men build more and more
fires than the day before. To the pursuers, it looked like reinforcements were pouring
in, his army swelling by the hour. Spooked by an enemy that seemed to be growing
unstoppable, they backed off. Yu Xu escaped — by faking strength he didn't have.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:24–1:38) ---

Fewer fires to look weak. More fires to look strong. Opposite moves — same genius. In
war, the real size of your army doesn't win the battle. What the enemy believes it is...
does.
