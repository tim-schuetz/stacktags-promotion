==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The revenge of Sun Bin. His jealous classmate Pang Juan lured him to Wei and had him
framed and crippled (kneecaps cut off — the punishment that gave him his name). Sun Bin
feigned insanity (ate filth, lived in a pigsty) to seem harmless, escaped to Qi, became
its master strategist, and engineered Pang Juan's death at Maling — a night ambush
sprung the instant Pang Juan lit a torch to read a message on a tree.
Punchline: the broken man out-thought the man who broke him.

SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — the payoff up front ---

A man was crippled and imprisoned by his own best friend. To escape, he pretended to go
insane — eating filth, sleeping with pigs — until his captor believed he was harmless.
Then he came back and engineered that friend's death, by torchlight. This really
happened.

--- THE BETRAYAL (≈0:16–0:44) ---

His name was Sun Bin. As a young man, he studied military strategy alongside a
classmate named Pang Juan. But Pang Juan became a successful general first — and he knew,
deep down, that Sun Bin was the more brilliant of the two. Jealousy curdled into
something monstrous. Pang Juan invited Sun Bin to his state, pretending friendship —
then had him framed on false charges. As punishment, Sun Bin's kneecaps were cut away,
crippling him for life, and his face was branded. The plan was to keep this genius
broken, hidden, and useful only to Pang Juan.

--- THE ESCAPE (≈0:44–1:08) ---

But Sun Bin had a weapon Pang Juan couldn't cut off: his mind. To get free, he made
everyone believe he'd lost it. He raved and babbled. He ate dirt and filth. He lived
among the pigs. He played the madman so convincingly that his guards stopped taking him
seriously — and eventually, agents from a rival state, Qi, smuggled the "harmless
lunatic" out. Once safe in Qi, the madness vanished, and Sun Bin became their chief
military strategist.

--- THE REVENGE (≈1:08–1:30) ---

Years later, the two faced each other on the battlefield — and Sun Bin set the perfect
trap. He lured Pang Juan's army, at night, to a narrow pass at a place called Maling.
There, he'd had a tree stripped of bark and a message carved into it. In the dark, Pang
Juan reached the tree, couldn't read it, and lit a torch to see the words. That flame was
the signal. The instant it flared, thousands of hidden Qi crossbowmen unleashed. Pang
Juan, trapped and defeated, took his own life — reportedly cursing that he'd made Sun
Bin famous.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:30–1:42) ---

The man who cut off Sun Bin's legs to control him ended up destroyed by the one thing he
could never cripple — Sun Bin's mind. The broken man out-thought the man who broke him.
