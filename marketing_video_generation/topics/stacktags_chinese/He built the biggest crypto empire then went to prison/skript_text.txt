Changpeng Zhao ("CZ"): born in China, raised in Canada; founded Binance in 2017, which
became the world's largest crypto exchange, making him one of the richest people alive.
In 2023 he pleaded guilty to US money-laundering / Bank Secrecy Act violations; Binance

(2024). He remained a multi-billionaire.
Punchline: he committed to the biggest crypto fine in history, did his time, and walked
out still one of the wealthiest people on Earth.

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — the payoff up front ---

This man built the biggest cryptocurrency company on the planet, became one of the
richest people alive, then pleaded guilty in the United States and went to prison. And
when he walked out a few months later? Still a billionaire many times over. Meet
Changpeng Zhao.

--- THE RISE (≈0:16–0:46) ---

Most people just call him CZ. He was born in China, moved to Canada as a kid, and worked
his way through the world of trading software. Then, in 2017, he founded a crypto
exchange called Binance — a place to buy and sell digital currencies. And it exploded.
Within just a few years, Binance became the largest crypto exchange on Earth, handling
enormous volumes of trades every single day. CZ, who owned a huge chunk of it, saw his
net worth rocket into the tens of billions. From a coding job to one of the richest
people on the planet, in well under a decade.

--- THE FALL (≈0:46–1:18) ---

But Binance had grown at breakneck speed — and US regulators argued it had cut serious
corners. The core accusation wasn't that CZ stole money. It was that Binance failed to
put proper controls in place to stop money laundering — letting illicit funds flow
through the platform. In 2023, it caught up with him. CZ pleaded guilty. Binance agreed
to pay around four billion dollars — one of the largest corporate settlements in US
history. CZ stepped down as CEO, paid a personal fine, and in 2024, he was sentenced to
prison. The founder of the world's biggest crypto empire, behind bars.

--- THE TWIST (≈1:18–1:36) ---

And here's the part that stuns people. His sentence was about four months. He served it,
walked out — and was still worth tens of billions of dollars. Because the value of his
stake in Binance dwarfed the fines. He'd broken the rules, paid a record penalty, done
his time... and emerged as one of the wealthiest human beings on Earth.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:36–1:48) ---

It's a story that says a lot about this era of money: you can build an empire at
lightning speed, collide head-on with the law, go to prison — and still come out the
other side a billionaire. Crypto's wild west, in one man's life.
