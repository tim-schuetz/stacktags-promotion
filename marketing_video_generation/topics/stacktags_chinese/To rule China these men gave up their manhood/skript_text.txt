Title: To reach the top in imperial China, men gave up their manhood
Topic: China / history
Target length: ~95 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Spoken voiceover narration only — no on-screen/visual cues.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
Eunuchs = castrated men who served in the imperial palace, especially the harem.
They were trusted precisely BECAUSE they couldn't father children or found a rival
dynasty. Many came from poor families who had a son castrated for a shot at a court
career; some self-castrated. A few gained enormous power. Practice ended with the
Qing's fall (1912).
Punchline: the one place a poor boy could touch absolute power — at the highest
possible price.

NOTE (honesty): castration was full removal; the removed parts ("the precious") were
kept to be buried with them. Keep it factual, not gratuitous.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:14) — the payoff up front ---

For two thousand years, there was a way for a poor Chinese boy to end up whispering in
the emperor's ear, running the palace, sometimes ruling the empire from the shadows.
The price of entry? He had to be castrated.

--- WHAT A EUNUCH WAS (≈0:14–0:36) ---

These were the eunuchs — men who'd been surgically castrated to serve inside the
imperial palace. And the reason they existed comes down to one room: the harem. An
emperor kept hundreds of women, and any child born there had to be unquestionably his.
So the only men allowed to live and work deep inside the palace were men who could
never father a child of their own.

--- WHY THEY WERE TRUSTED (≈0:36–1:04) ---

And that's exactly why emperors trusted them with everything. A normal official might
scheme to put his own son on the throne, to start his own dynasty. A eunuch couldn't.
He had no heirs, no bloodline, no future beyond the emperor he served. That made him,
in theory, the safest servant in the world — so eunuchs ended up guarding the
emperor's secrets, his money, his access. And whoever controls access to a god-emperor
controls a great deal. Some eunuchs grew so powerful they toppled ministers, and even
made and unmade emperors.

--- WHERE THEY CAME FROM (≈1:04–1:24) ---

So who would choose this? Often, desperate families. For a poor boy with no land and no
prospects, the palace was the one ladder to wealth and power that existed — and some
parents had a son castrated young, betting his whole body on that one chance. Others
castrated themselves as adults, hoping to buy their way in. They even kept the removed
parts preserved, to be buried with them, so they could go to the afterlife "whole."

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:24–1:38) ---

The system only ended when the last dynasty fell, just over a century ago. For two
millennia, it was the strangest bargain in Chinese history: give up the one thing that
made you a man — for the one shot at standing beside the most powerful man alive.
