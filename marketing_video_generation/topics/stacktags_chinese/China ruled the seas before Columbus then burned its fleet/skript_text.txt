VIDEO SCRIPT
Title: China ruled the seas 90 years before Columbus — then burned its own fleet
Topic: China / history
Target length: ~100 seconds (fits the 30s–3min window)
Language of narration: English
Format: Spoken voiceover narration only — no on-screen/visual cues.

==================================================================
THE CORE IDEA
==================================================================
The most famous eunuch: Admiral Zheng He. A castrated Muslim slave who rose to command
the Ming "treasure fleet" — 7 enormous voyages (1405–1433) reaching SE Asia, India,
Arabia, and East Africa, with ships dwarfing later European ones. Then China abruptly
ended the voyages, turned inward, and let the fleet rot — decades before Europe's age
of exploration began.
Punchline: China could have "discovered" the world first — and chose to sail home and
shut the door.

NOTE (honesty): exact ship sizes are debated (sources cite very large "treasure
ships"); say "far larger than" European caravels rather than exact metres.

==================================================================
SCRIPT
==================================================================

--- HOOK (0:00–0:16) — the payoff up front ---

Almost a century before Columbus, China had the most powerful navy on Earth — gigantic
ships sailing all the way to Africa. And then it did something almost unbelievable: it
called the fleet home, let it rot, and turned its back on the ocean. And the man who
led it all was a castrated slave.

--- WHO WAS ZHENG HE (≈0:16–0:42) ---

His name was Zheng He. Born a Muslim boy in Yunnan, he was captured as a child by the
Ming army and castrated — made a eunuch servant. But he was brilliant, and loyal, and
he rose. By his thirties, the emperor handed him command of the largest fleet the
world had ever seen: the treasure fleet. We're talking hundreds of ships and tens of
thousands of men — with the flagships built on a scale that dwarfed the little
caravels Europe would send out decades later.

--- THE VOYAGES (≈0:42–1:08) ---

Between 1405 and 1433, Zheng He led seven epic voyages. He sailed across Southeast
Asia, to India, to Arabia, and down the coast of East Africa — trading, showing the
flag, collecting tribute, and bringing exotic wonders back to the emperor. China, at
that moment, could project power and wealth right across the known world. It was poised
to "discover" everything Europe later would — and it got there first.

--- THE TURN INWARD (≈1:08–1:30) ---

And then... it just stopped. After Zheng He, the court changed its mind. Powerful
officials decided the voyages were a vain, ruinous waste — money better spent on land
and walls. So the expeditions were cancelled. The great ships were left to rot in their
docks. Records of the voyages were destroyed. And building a ship that big was even
made a crime. China deliberately sailed home and bolted the door to the sea — right as
Europe was about to fling its own doors open.

--- PUNCHLINE / OUTRO (≈1:30–1:44) ---

Imagine how different history might look if China had kept sailing. Instead, the
greatest navy in the world was led by a eunuch, reached the edge of Africa — and was
then quietly burned by the very empire that built it.
